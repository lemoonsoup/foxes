    • Early Life

  • A baby fox is called a cub, kit or pup.
  • A group of baby foxes is called a litter.
  • Newborn fox cubs are blind, and deaf.
  • When a fox cub is born their fur will be dark-grey.
  • A litter of foxes usually has 4-5 cubs.
  • Baby foxes can forage for food after only 12 weeks

    • Personality

  • While most Fox species tend to live in packs, some, like the Arctic Fox, tend to be solitary.
  • They communicate with other pack members using a set of vocalizations. Whining, yelping, high-pitched explosive howling, sharp barking, growling, and a warning bark are examples of these sounds.
  • Foxes are extremely playful.
  • Foxes are extremely adaptable.

    • Features

  • Foxes can make over 40 different sounds.
  • Foxes have whiskers on their legs and face, which help them to navigate
  • Grey foxes can retract their claws like cats do
  • A fox can run up to a speed of 30 miles an hour
  • Foxes use their tails for thermoregulation

    • Population

  • In the wild, the average Fox lives for up to 5 years, meanwhile Foxes in captivity tend to live for up to 10 years.
  • A group of Foxes is called a leash, skulk, or eath.
  • Foxes are classified into six genera and have 23 living species.
  • There are between 9,840 and 19,200 foxes left in the world.
  • The red fox is the most common fox.
  • One of the rarest fox types is the Sierra Nevada Red Fox, with only a few hundred of them estimated to be left in the wild.

    • Facts

  • The Red Fox is the largest species of Foxes, while the Fennec Fox is the smallest.
  • A male is called a ‘dog fox’ while a female is called a ‘vixen’
  • The Grey Fox from North America is the only breed of fox that can climb trees
  • Their tail is over half its body length.
  • Like cats, foxes are nocturnal, which means that they go about most of their business at night.
  • Over 20 species of foxes can be found inhabiting every continent of the world, except for Antarctica.
